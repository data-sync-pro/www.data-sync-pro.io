A Self-Adaptive Trigger in DSP is a trigger-based automation that transforms and validates fields within the same records before they are committed to the database. Unlike standard trigger actions that perform operations on related records, Self-Adaptive Triggers focus on making real-time modifications to the triggering records themselves.

When a record is inserted or updated, DSP evaluates predefined formulas and transformations to dynamically update, validate, or re-calculate fields based on business logic. These operations occur before or after the DML event, ensuring that records are properly modified before being saved.
